## 1. Purpose and Scope

1.1. This Acceptable Use Policy ("AUP") governs your use of CrowByte Terminal ("the Software"), developed and published by HLSITech (doing business as CrowByte). The AUP applies to all integrated security testing capabilities, including but not limited to network scanning, vulnerability assessment, exploitation frameworks, remote access tools, AI-assisted reconnaissance, and any other offensive or defensive security functionality provided by the Software.

1.2. CrowByte Terminal is designed exclusively for authorized security testing, penetration testing, red team operations, vulnerability assessment, and defensive security operations conducted by qualified security professionals.

1.3. By installing, accessing, or using the Software, you acknowledge that you have read, understood, and agree to be bound by this AUP, the End User License Agreement ("EULA"), the Privacy Policy, and all applicable laws and regulations. If you do not agree to these terms, you must not install, access, or use the Software.

1.4. This AUP applies to all users of the Software, whether individual practitioners, employees of organizations, or contractors performing security assessments on behalf of third parties.

## 2. Definitions

2.1. **"Authorized Testing"** means security testing, scanning, assessment, or exploitation activities performed on Target Systems for which the User has obtained explicit, documented authorization from the System Owner, and which is conducted within a defined scope and in compliance with all applicable laws.

2.2. **"CrowByte"** means HLSITech, doing business as CrowByte, including its officers, directors, employees, agents, affiliates, successors, and assigns.

2.3. **"Good Faith Security Research"** means accessing a computer solely for purposes of good-faith testing, investigation, and/or correction of a security flaw or vulnerability, where such activity is carried out in a manner designed to avoid any harm to individuals or the public, and where the information derived from the activity is used primarily to promote the security or safety of the class of devices, machines, or online services to which the accessed computer belongs, or those who use such devices, machines, or online services. This definition is aligned with the U.S. Department of Justice and HackerOne Gold Standard Safe Harbor definitions.

2.4. **"Rules of Engagement" ("ROE")** means the written document specifying the scope, methods, timing, boundaries, emergency contacts, and limitations of an authorized security testing engagement.

2.5. **"Sanctioned Country"** means any country or territory subject to comprehensive sanctions under the U.S. Office of Foreign Assets Control ("OFAC"), including but not limited to Cuba, Iran, North Korea, Syria, and the Crimea, Donetsk, and Luhansk regions of Ukraine, or any jurisdiction subject to equivalent sanctions by the European Union, United Kingdom, Canada, or Australia.

2.6. **"Software"** means CrowByte Terminal, including all modules, updates, patches, extensions, documentation, and any associated services provided by CrowByte.

2.7. **"System Owner"** means the individual or entity with legal authority and operational control over the Target Systems, including the right to authorize third-party security testing.

2.8. **"Target Systems"** means the specific networks, systems, IP addresses, domains, applications, APIs, and infrastructure identified in a written authorization document as within scope for security testing.

2.9. **"User" or "You"** means any individual or entity that installs, accesses, or uses the Software.

## 3. Authorized Use

3.1. **Authorization Required.** You shall use the Software only to scan, test, assess, or exploit systems for which you have obtained explicit, written authorization from the System Owner. You represent and warrant that you possess the appropriate authorizations for all networks, systems, IP addresses, assets, and hardware that you target, scan, monitor, or test using the Software.

3.2. **Scope Compliance.** All testing activities must be conducted within the scope defined in your written authorization or Rules of Engagement. You shall not exceed the authorized scope, access systems beyond the defined boundaries, or perform testing activities not covered by your authorization.

3.3. **Documentation Requirements.** You shall maintain complete records of all authorizations, Rules of Engagement, scope documents, and written permissions for the duration of any engagement and for a minimum of three (3) years thereafter. These records must be producible upon request in the event of a dispute, audit, or investigation.

3.4. **Third-Party Infrastructure.** If Target Systems include infrastructure hosted by, managed by, or belonging to third parties — including cloud service providers, hosting providers, SaaS platforms, content delivery networks, or managed service providers — you are responsible for obtaining all necessary additional authorizations from such third parties before commencing testing. Authorization from the System Owner alone is not sufficient for testing infrastructure that the System Owner does not own or control.

3.5. **Professional Competence.** You represent that you possess the technical knowledge and professional competence necessary to use the Software responsibly and to understand the potential impact of security testing activities on Target Systems and their surrounding environments.

3.6. **Permitted Activities.** The Software may be used for:

   (a) Authorized penetration testing and red team engagements;

   (b) Vulnerability assessment of systems you own or are authorized to test;

   (c) Security auditing and compliance testing within a defined scope;

   (d) Defensive security operations and blue team exercises;

   (e) Security research on systems you own or within controlled laboratory environments;

   (f) Participation in bug bounty and vulnerability disclosure programs, in compliance with the applicable program rules;

   (g) Educational and training purposes on systems you own or are authorized to use.

## 4. Prohibited Use

4.1. **Unauthorized Access.** You shall NOT use the Software to access, scan, test, exploit, or otherwise interact with any computer, network, system, application, or service for which you do not have explicit, written authorization from the System Owner. This prohibition applies regardless of your intent, including purportedly benign or research-oriented purposes.

4.2. **Illegal Activity.** You shall NOT use the Software for any purpose that violates applicable local, state, national, or international laws or regulations, including but not limited to:

   (a) The Computer Fraud and Abuse Act ("CFAA"), 18 U.S.C. Section 1030 (United States);

   (b) The Computer Misuse Act 1990 (United Kingdom);

   (c) Criminal Code Section 342.1 (Canada);

   (d) Applicable EU member state cybercrime legislation implementing the Convention on Cybercrime (Budapest Convention);

   (e) Any other applicable computer crime, data protection, or privacy legislation in the jurisdiction(s) where testing occurs or where Target Systems are located.

4.3. **Malicious Activity.** You shall NOT use the Software to:

   (a) Deploy ransomware, cryptominers, wipers, or other destructive malware;

   (b) Conduct denial-of-service ("DoS") or distributed denial-of-service ("DDoS") attacks;

   (c) Exfiltrate, steal, sell, or publicly disclose personal data, credentials, financial information, trade secrets, or other confidential information;

   (d) Engage in extortion, blackmail, or coercion;

   (e) Conduct espionage or unauthorized surveillance;

   (f) Facilitate fraud, identity theft, or financial crime;

   (g) Interfere with critical infrastructure, emergency services, healthcare systems, or any systems where disruption could endanger human life or public safety;

   (h) Conduct "hack back," retaliatory, or offensive counter-operations against any party.

4.4. **Scope Violations.** You shall NOT:

   (a) Test systems beyond the authorized scope;

   (b) Access data beyond what is strictly necessary to demonstrate a vulnerability;

   (c) Retain copies of sensitive data obtained during testing beyond the minimum necessary for reporting purposes;

   (d) Pivot to or exploit systems that were not explicitly included in the Rules of Engagement;

   (e) Conduct testing outside of authorized time windows.

4.5. **Circumvention of Software Controls.** You shall NOT use the Software to circumvent, disable, or undermine the security controls of the Software itself, including license verification, usage tracking, telemetry systems, or any technical protection measures.

4.6. **Redistribution.** You shall NOT redistribute, sublicense, sell, lease, rent, or otherwise transfer the Software or any component thereof to any third party without explicit written permission from CrowByte.

## 8. Responsible Disclosure and Vulnerability Reporting

8.1. **Responsible Disclosure Obligation.** If, during authorized use of the Software, you discover a vulnerability in a Target System, you shall report such vulnerability to the System Owner in accordance with:

   (a) The Rules of Engagement for the specific engagement;

   (b) The vulnerability disclosure policy of the organization operating the Target System;

   (c) Applicable bug bounty program rules (e.g., HackerOne, Bugcrowd, Intigriti);

   (d) In the absence of specific guidance, industry-standard responsible disclosure practices.

8.2. **Disclosure Timeline.** You shall provide the System Owner a reasonable remediation period before any public disclosure of vulnerability details. In the absence of a specific agreed timeline, a minimum of ninety (90) days from the date of the initial report is recommended, consistent with prevailing industry norms.

8.3. **No Exploitative Disclosure.** You shall NOT disclose vulnerability information for the purpose of extortion, coercion, embarrassment, competitive advantage, or any purpose other than improving the security posture of the affected systems.

8.4. **CrowByte Vulnerabilities.** If you discover a vulnerability in the Software itself or in any CrowByte-operated system, you shall report it to CrowByte at security@crowbyte.io before any public disclosure. CrowByte commits to acknowledge such reports within five (5) business days and to work toward remediation in good faith.

## 9. Data Handling and Privacy

9.1. **Minimal Data Access.** During authorized testing, you shall access only the minimum data necessary to demonstrate the existence of a vulnerability. You shall not access, copy, modify, or exfiltrate personal data, financial records, medical records, trade secrets, or other sensitive information beyond what is strictly necessary for your security assessment.

9.2. **Data Retention.** Any data obtained during testing shall be retained only for the duration necessary to complete the engagement and deliver the agreed-upon report. All test data, screenshots, logs, and artifacts containing sensitive information shall be securely deleted upon completion of the engagement, unless retention is required by applicable law, regulation, or the engagement contract.

9.3. **Data Protection Compliance.** You shall comply with all applicable data protection and privacy laws with respect to any personal data encountered during testing, including but not limited to:

   (a) The General Data Protection Regulation ("GDPR") (European Union);

   (b) The California Consumer Privacy Act ("CCPA") and the California Privacy Rights Act ("CPRA") (California, United States);

   (c) The Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act ("PIPEDA") (Canada);

   (d) The Data Protection Act 2018 (United Kingdom);

   (e) Any other applicable data protection legislation in the jurisdiction(s) where the data subjects reside or where testing occurs.

9.4. **Incident Notification.** If you inadvertently access sensitive personal data during authorized testing, you shall immediately notify the System Owner and cooperate fully in any required breach notification or incident response procedures.
